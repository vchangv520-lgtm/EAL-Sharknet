The misconception

A shark net is not a wall.

People picture a protective barrier across the beach. In reality a net is only a section of mesh offshore — sharks swim over it, under it and around it.

NSW has used these nets since 1937. They have never been a total barrier — yet they make the public feel protected.

Source: ABC News

The hidden danger

Looking safe isn't the same as being safe.

A method that only looks protective gives the public a false sense of security — and the ocean still isn't walled off.
